## Active Hypotheses

- **H1:** The RGM buyer is easier to close than the eCommerce/retail media buyer because RGM has the budget allocation problem that DSIM directly solves.
  - Status: Unconfirmed — needs 3+ deal data points
  - Supporting: Skai 2026 survey confirms 75% of advertisers can't operationalize incrementality insights (the RGM gap); incremental spending signal shows finance/CFO buyers are increasingly engaged in retail media ROI decisions
  - Counter signal: None yet from deal experience

- **H2:** P&G, PepsiCo, and Unilever are the best initial targets because one corporate deal unlocks multiple brands simultaneously.
  - Status: Unconfirmed — depends on DataWeave's existing relationships
  - Supporting: Plan-of-action.md outreach prioritization logic; parent company procurement model is real
  - Counter signal: Clorox and Mondelēz named as priority targets in GTM plan (smaller but faster to close); Mondelēz is already a CommerceIQ customer ($4M wasted media case study) — DSIM complement angle exists

- **H3:** "No activation conflict" positioning resonates most strongly with brands who are already suspicious of Publicis/agency-owned measurement tools.
  - Status: Partially confirmed by research
  - Supporting: Profitero's Hofstetter departure (March 2026) means Publicis independence narrative is gone; Publicis now fully in control; FTC regulatory action on Publicis (April 2026) adds compliance signal for sensitive buyers
  - Still needs: customer call signal to confirm this resonates vs. just being logically correct

- **H4:** CommerceIQ deal displacement requires a PoC-first motion — brands won't rip-and-replace without seeing DSIM's MAPE/R-squared vs. CommerceIQ's undisclosed methodology.
  - Status: Supported but not confirmed from deal experience
  - Update: CommerceIQ launched standalone Incrementality Module (Dec 2025) — this means some accounts may have CIQ measurement but not CIQ activation. In those accounts, DSIM competes head-to-head on methodology (DSIM: Bayesian MMM, published MAPE/R²; CIQ: observational model, nothing published). PoC motion still correct but head-to-head methodology comparison may be possible in measurement-only deals.

- **H5:** Traditional MMM vendors (Analytic Partners, Circana) do not actively compete for the same deal as DSIM — they compete for different budget lines and buyer personas.
  - Status: Likely confirmed (not from DSIM deal data, but from structural analysis)
  - Supporting: AP/Circana owned by Finance/CFO; DSIM targets RGM/eCommerce. Different budget lines confirmed by Skai's 2026 research showing measurement capability gap across the industry — buyers are not choosing between AP and DSIM; they might use both.
  - Still needs: sales team validation from actual deal experiences
