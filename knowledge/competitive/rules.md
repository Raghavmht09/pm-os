# competitive — rules
Last updated: 2026-04-24

Rules confirmed from live research (April 2026 deep-dive + PM OS subagent runs). Each rule cites the source event that confirmed it.

---

## Rule 1: No competitor surfaces shelf signals as actionable outputs in their incrementality model
**Source:** Live research April 2026 — all 6 competitors verified.
- CommerceIQ: no shelf signals in Incrementality Estimation Module (keyword/propensity model only)
- Pacvue: inventory listed as one input, not surfaced as output; no SoS, content, competitive pricing
- Incremental.com: explicitly uses search rank, buy box, inventory as CONTROL VARIABLES to neutralize confounders — not as deliverables
- Profitero: monitoring heritage; SIM uses shelf signals as bid triggers, not causal attribution variables
- Skai: Profitero SIM integration is reactive optimization only; Impact Navigator has no shelf-signal inputs
- Analytic Partners / Measured / Haus: national/aggregate media models only

**Implication:** DSIM's "shelf-native diagnostic" quadrant is structurally uncontested. This rule holds until a competitor explicitly adds shelf signals as first-class output variables.
**Watch signal:** CommerceIQ standalone Incrementality Module launch (Dec 2025) is the nearest threat — monitor quarterly for methodology changes.

## Rule 2: Walmart store-cluster localization is uncontested across all measurement and activation platforms
**Source:** Live research April 2026 — confirmed across all 6 competitors.
- CommerceIQ: keyword-level only; no store-cluster
- Pacvue: national/campaign level; no geo-lift or store-cluster documentation
- Incremental.com: national or campaign level; no geographic granularity
- Skai: geo-based testing exists but city-pair/DMA level; not Walmart behavioral store-cluster
- Analytic Partners: national MMM
- Measured/Haus: geo-lift at city/DMA level, not Walmart behavioral store cluster

**Implication:** 17-cluster behavioral segmentation is a unique DSIM capability. Lead with this in any deal involving a CPG brand with regional distribution variance at Walmart.

## Rule 3: CommerceIQ's methodology is observational — Walmart Connect's own tools publicly refute it
**Source:** CommerceIQ Nov 2025 blog + Walmart Connect April 2025 Search Incrementality launch (verified April 2026).
- CIQ's blog: model focuses on "search and keyword-based incrementality, based on 14-day direct sales conversions"
- Walmart Connect April 2025: *"gold-standard test-and-control methodology backed by real, verified sales data"* (explicitly positioned against "third-party data or observational models")
- CIQ's iROAS standalone module (Dec 2025) uses the same observational approach in the standalone product

**Implication:** In deals where CommerceIQ is the comparison, bring the Walmart Connect quote. "Walmart Connect itself says observational models aren't the gold standard for their platform. Here's DSIM's MAPE and R² — show me CIQ's equivalent."

## Rule 5: Profitero's measurement capability requires Pacvue as the measurement engine — it is not native
**Source:** Panasonic case study on profitero.com (verified April 2026): *"Pacvue's Incrementality Console allowed Panasonic to measure and validate incremental uplift."* Profitero+ bundles Mars United Commerce managed services.
**Implication:** In any deal where Profitero is in: "Profitero tells you what changed on your shelf. DSIM tells you what each change was worth. That's a question Profitero cannot answer even with Pacvue in the stack — because Pacvue's model doesn't connect shelf signals to sales causation."

## Rule 7: Incremental.com is a warm prospect signal, not a displacement threat
**Source:** Incremental.com product analysis (incremental.com/how-it-works, April 2026).
Incremental uses shelf signals to clean the media signal. Brands using Incremental are already sold on the idea that shelf dynamics affect media outcomes. They need the shelf execution data to act on — which DSIM provides.
**Implication:** When a brand says "we use Incremental.com," the correct response is: "Great — Incremental tells you how much media worked. DSIM tells you what shelf conditions constrained it and what fixing them is worth. These are complementary, not overlapping."

## Rule 8: Incrementality awareness is high; capability is thin — 75% of advertisers say it's their #1 challenge
**Source:** Skai 2026 State of Retail Media Measurement and Incrementality (April 2026 research).
- 75% cite incrementality as biggest measurement challenge
- Only 20% proficient at both measuring AND applying insights
- 50% measure at a basic level only

**Implication:** The market is primed. Buyers know they have a problem. The pitch is not education about incrementality — it's evidence that DSIM solves it better than what they already tried.
