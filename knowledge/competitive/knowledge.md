## DSIM Competitive Landscape (Walmart)
Source: `insider-data/battlecards/DSIM_Competitive_BattleCards_1.pptx`

### Threat Map
DSIM sits at the intersection of three markets — digital shelf analytics, retail media activation, and MMM/incrementality measurement. No single competitor owns all three.

| Tier | Competitors | Core tension with DSIM |
|---|---|---|
| Direct (HIGH) | CommerceIQ, Profitero, Ascential Edge/NIQ, Intelligence Node | Same shelf-data foundation, racing to add incrementality |
| Retail Media (MEDIUM) | Pacvue, Skai, Flywheel, Quartile, Perpetua | Own Walmart Connect activation; adding measurement layers |
| MMM Pure-play (STRUCTURAL) | Analytic Partners, Nielsen, Circana, Ekimetrics, Measured/Haus | Own the CFO conversation; slow and shelf-blind |
| In-house (SPECIAL) | Walmart Connect | Free, deterministic, but narrow — only measures Walmart media |

### Closest 1:1 Competitor
**CommerceIQ** — explicitly optimizes for iROAS, same narrative as DSIM. Closed-loop automation + 50+ real-time signals. Weakness: tethered to its activation stack, not shelf-native.

### DSIM Differentiation (per deck)
1. Only impact model built on top of a production-grade digital shelf platform — availability, content, pricing, sponsored visibility are first-class inputs, not afterthoughts
2. ~5.9% MAPE forecast accuracy with high R-squared — credible with finance/CFO audience
3. Walmart-native focus — not a multi-retailer diluted product

### Key Positioning Gaps to Exploit per Competitor
- **Profitero:** broad coverage, weak on causal measurement — "owns coverage, not causation"
- **Pacvue:** owns activation, not measurement — "owns Walmart Connect, not the shelf"
- **Analytic Partners / Nielsen:** CFO trust, shelf-blind and slow — quarterly deliverables vs. real-time
- **Walmart Connect:** free and deterministic but narrow — only answers "how incremental is my Walmart media?" not the full shelf story
