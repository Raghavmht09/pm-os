## Why this exists

Confluence does not define target personas for the AI Wrapper. Only the stale Jul 2025 AA doc names "category manager"; PI docs say "pricing analyst"; MM docs say "data ops"; "ecommerce lead" appears nowhere. The strategy v1 doc anchored on category manager + ecommerce lead. PM has now extended the persona set to **Finance executive · Category manager · Ecommerce lead** × **Retailer-side · Brand-side** — six cells.

This matrix grounds each cell in (a) what Confluence already implies the user does inside PI/AA/MM and (b) desk research on day-to-day cadence and KPIs. Each entry is tagged `[CONFLUENCE-GROUNDED]` or `[WEB-RESEARCH]` so the PM can validate before nudges or agents are persona-targeted. Cells with thin evidence are flagged `[THIN — CSM VALIDATION NEEDED]`.

The retailer-vs-brand split matters because the same job title means different things on each side. NielsenIQ / "Making Business Matter" research is explicit: retailer category managers are paid to grow **categories** (margin, GMROI, share of wallet inside the store); brand category managers are paid to grow **brands** inside someone else's store (distribution, share of shelf, MAP compliance). The AI surface needs to know which side it's talking to before it phrases a nudge.

1. **Concept A (nudges):** Use the "AI chat trigger moments" column verbatim to draft persona-anchored nudge copy per module. Phrase retailer-side nudges in category language; brand-side in brand/share-of-shelf language.
2. **Concept B (MCP):** Brand-side ecommerce lead is the single highest-fit MCP consumer (calls into DataWeave from their own BI). Prioritize the share-of-shelf + price-feed tools first.
3. **Concept C (agent canvas):** Internal CSM playbooks per persona × side. Each cell above implies a starter template.
4. **Trust Layer publication (§1.6 ask #4):** Both finance executive rows depend on it. Without published Trust Layer, finance veto is the default outcome at renewal `[WEB-RESEARCH: Adobe CX, Sage B2B]`.
