# BATTLECARD 1: The eCommerce / Retail Media Leader

## Who They Are
**Title:** VP eCommerce, Director of Retail Media, Senior Manager Walmart Channel
**Reports to:** Chief eCommerce Officer or VP Sales
**Owns:** Walmart Connect budget ($500K–$5M+/yr), digital shelf execution, campaign performance
**Tools they use today:** Walmart Connect dashboards, Scintilla, Pacvue or Skai (via agency), Stackline or Profitero for shelf monitoring

## What They're Measured On
1. Walmart.com net sales (primary)
2. ROAS / iROAS on Walmart Connect
3. Share of Search in category (tracked weekly)
4. In-stock rate during campaign windows
5. Walmart JBP target attainment

## The Problem They Can't Solve Today
Their Walmart Connect ROAS dropped last quarter. Or it looks fine but sales didn't move. They don't know whether that's a media problem (wrong channels, wrong keywords) or a shelf problem (72% in-stock rate eating their impressions, a content score that tanks conversion after the click). They're blamed for campaign underperformance that may have nothing to do with how the campaigns were run.

## How to Open the Conversation
*"When your Walmart Connect campaign underperforms, how do you know whether it's a media problem or a shelf problem — and which one to fix first?"*

If they say "I don't" or "we look at ROAS" → qualified.

## What DSIM Does for Them
- Decomposes their total Walmart sales across 6 levers (media spend, Share of Search, in-stock, content, pricing, competitive pressure) — so they can see *what* drove or capped the last campaign
- Shows which of their 17 store clusters are producing the best media response vs. which are suppressed by availability or content issues
- Tells them explicitly: "Move $22K/day from Onsite Search (0.83 iROI) to Offsite Display (1.55 iROI) to maximize lift within your current total budget"

## One-Liner
*"Your Walmart Connect ROAS is what the platform says happened. DSIM shows what actually drove the sale — and which shelf problem capped the lift you paid for."*

## Top 3 Objections and Responses
**"I already have Walmart Connect dashboards / Scintilla / Pacvue."**
→ "Those tell you what correlated with sales. DSIM tells you what caused them. None of those tools show you whether your 72% in-stock rate suppressed your campaign lift — or whether moving budget to Offsite Display would have outperformed Onsite Search. They're inputs. DSIM is the model that connects them."

**"This sounds like a 3-month consulting engagement."**
→ "PoC is 6–8 weeks. Tableau delivery. Lactalis got model-ready output in under 10 weeks and identified $30K/day in reallocation opportunity. That pays for a $75K PoC before delivery closes."

**"I don't control my shelf metrics — that's supply chain."**
→ "True. But DSIM gives you the dollar value of fixing it. That's the business case to take to supply chain. Right now you're telling them 'our in-stock rate is low.' After DSIM, you're telling them 'our in-stock rate is costing us $X/week in media lift.'"

## Warm Prospect Signals
- Uses Incremental.com and keeps finding unexplained variance in lift by week
- Has been asked to justify Walmart Connect budget to Finance and struggled
- Just had a campaign underperform and can't explain why
- Is preparing for a Walmart JBP review and wants independent data

# BATTLECARD 2: The Finance / Revenue Growth Management (RGM) Leader

## Who They Are
**Title:** VP Revenue Growth Management, Director Commercial Finance, VP Trade Marketing
**Reports to:** CFO or Chief Commercial Officer
**Owns:** Budget allocation decisions across channels, trade spend, pricing, JBP financial commitments
**Tools they use today:** NielsenIQ / Circana for category trends, MMM outputs (quarterly), Scintilla-sourced sales summaries from eCommerce team, internal P&L models

## What They're Measured On
1. Gross margin / trade spend efficiency
2. Incremental revenue per trade dollar invested
3. JBP target achievement
4. Accuracy of media ROI attribution (for CFO conversations)
5. Category contribution to company P&L

## The Problem They Can't Solve Today
The eCommerce team keeps asking for more Walmart Connect budget. Finance has no independent way to verify whether those requests are justified or whether the constraint is a shelf problem (OOS, poor content) that more media won't fix. Their existing measurement (MMM, Circana) doesn't capture retail media incrementality with enough accuracy to push back. They're forced to approve based on competitive necessity or internal politics, not causal data.

## How to Open the Conversation
*"When your eCommerce team asks for more Walmart Connect budget, what evidence do they bring — and do you have an independent source to evaluate it against?"*

If they say "we mostly accept what the platform says" or "it's a competitive necessity" → qualified.

## What DSIM Does for Them
- Provides vendor-neutral, independent attribution of what drove Walmart sales — not platform ROAS, not agency reporting, not MMM extrapolation. The number they can take to the CFO.
- Answers the JBP question directly: "Is our Walmart Connect spend generating truly incremental sales, or are we paying for sales that would have happened anyway?"
- Identifies whether the eCommerce team's next budget request is justified (media is the constraint) or whether fixing availability or content would deliver more incremental return per dollar
- Shows the explicit reallocation: "move $X from Channel A to Channel B — here's the expected lift and confidence interval"

## One-Liner
*"Before you approve the next Walmart Connect budget request, DSIM shows whether the bottleneck is media spend or in-stock rate — and where the dollar goes further."*

## Top 3 Objections and Responses
**"We use Analytic Partners / Circana for MMM — we already have measurement."**
→ "AP gives you the quarterly picture at the brand level. DSIM gives you the weekly shelf-level picture at the store cluster level with 5.9% MAPE vs. AP's 10–15% on the retail media attribution layer. IAB's November 2025 guidelines explicitly rate MMM as 'moderate to weak causal strength' for retail media — the category body says MMM wasn't built for this. DSIM was."

**"The data is too granular for my team to act on."**
→ "The output is a single Tableau view: here's what to reallocate and here's the dollar impact. We built for the decision, not the raw data. The 17 store clusters are aggregated to the recommendation layer — you see the insight, not the model."

**"I don't have budget for another measurement tool."**
→ "This is a $50–75K PoC scoped as a project, not a SaaS subscription. Category manager budget line, not IT procurement. Lactalis identified $30K/day in reallocation opportunity — that's $10M+/year identified. At 1.5% of identified opportunity, the PoC pays for itself before delivery. And you get the first number your CFO can defend that doesn't come from the platform being measured."

## Warm Prospect Signals
- Has been asked by their CFO to independently verify eCommerce's media ROI claims
- Is frustrated that MMM outputs don't match what Walmart Connect reports
- Has a JBP coming up where Walmart is pushing for more media spend commitment
- Has seen eCommerce budget grow YoY without clear evidence of proportional sales growth

# BATTLECARD 3: The Brand / Category Manager

## Who They Are
**Title:** Senior Brand Manager, Category Director, VP Marketing (Walmart-focused)
**Reports to:** CMO or VP Brand
**Owns:** Brand Walmart P&L, promotional calendar, new SKU launch success, competitive response
**Tools they use today:** NielsenIQ / Circana (6–8 week lag), Profitero or DataWeave DSA for shelf monitoring, brand's own promotional analytics

## What They're Measured On
1. Category market share at Walmart.com
2. SKU velocity (units per store per week)
3. Distribution / ACV on new items
4. Promotional ROI (lift during display / price events)
5. Competitive defense (holding position vs. gaining competitors)

## The Problem They Can't Solve Today
By the time NielsenIQ tells them a competitor took two points of share, it happened 6 weeks ago. They monitor digital shelf metrics but can't connect shelf changes to sales impact — they don't know whether their 3-point SoS loss this month is worth responding to, or whether a competitor's new SKU launch is cannibalizing their category or creating incremental demand. They're reacting to signals they can't quantify.

## How to Open the Conversation
*"When a competitor gains Share of Search in your category on Walmart, how do you know whether it's worth responding to — and what responding is worth in sales terms?"*

## What DSIM Does for Them
- Connects digital shelf changes (competitor's SoS gaining, new SKU appearing, pricing moves) to predicted sales impact — weeks before scanner data catches up
- Quantifies the competitive threat: "Competitor X gained 4% SoS in the past 6 weeks. DSIM estimates this is suppressing your Walmart.com sales by $X/week. Here are the 4 store clusters where the impact is most concentrated."
- Validates new SKU launches: "Your new SKU had strong media support but 67% content compliance at launch. DSIM estimates this content gap cost $X in the first 30 days."
- Surfaces sub-brand halo effects: "Your Kids/Baby media spend is generating +12% incremental lift in the Adult sub-brand. This is likely the most undervalued line in your promotional calendar."

## One-Liner
*"By the time Nielsen tells you a competitor took two points of share, it happened 6 weeks ago. DSIM shows you the shelf signal that preceded it — while there's still time to respond."*

## Top 3 Objections and Responses
**"Profitero already gives us shelf intelligence."**
→ "Profitero tells you what changed — SoS is up, content score is down. DSIM tells you what each change was worth in dollars. And Profitero is now fully inside Publicis — is that the neutral number you take into the JBP? DSIM has no agency stake."

**"This is too granular for brand decisions — we work at the category level."**
→ "The 17 store clusters are an option, not a requirement. We can aggregate to regional or national views. But most brands find the cluster view is where the insight lives — a national 0% share gain hides a -5% in Texas and +5% in the Northeast. The national number is the average of two very different situations."

**"We don't have the budget for this right now."**
→ "The PoC is $50–75K. It's a project, not a subscription. And we scope it to your highest-priority question — competitor response, new SKU launch validation, promotional ROI. One question, one PoC, one actionable output."

## Why This Is a Secondary Persona
The brand team benefits from DSIM outputs but is less likely to be the engagement sponsor. The eCommerce team or Finance/RGM team typically initiates and funds the PoC. Position DSIM to the primary buyers (Personas 1 and 2) and include the brand team use case to show organizational breadth.

## Warm Prospect Signals
- Has a competitor gaining shelf position and doesn't know the dollar impact
- Just launched a new SKU and is trying to understand early Walmart performance signals
- Is preparing for JBP and wants to show Walmart evidence of shelf investment value
- Uses DataWeave DSA and already sees SoS / content / availability data — just doesn't have the causal layer connecting it to sales

## Digital Shelf Summit Conversation Guide

**For Brian and Karthik — 3 qualifying questions to use in any DSIM conversation:**

1. *"When your Walmart Connect campaign underperforms, how do you know whether it's a media problem or a shelf problem?"*
   - If they say "I don't" → Persona 1 (eCommerce buyer)
   - If they say "I don't control the campaigns" → Persona 2 (Finance/RGM buyer)

2. *"Who in your organization has to approve a Walmart Connect budget increase — and what data do they need to see?"*
   - If Finance/RGM approval is mentioned → Persona 2 conversation
   - If eCommerce owns the budget → Persona 1 conversation

3. *"Do you have sub-brands where one brand's media spend might be lifting a sister brand's organic sales — and is that in your current attribution?"*
   - If yes to the setup, no to the attribution → warm lead for either persona

**What NOT to promise at the Summit:**
- No keyword discovery (not in current product)
- No explicit cross-channel reallocation from a Tableau dashboard yet (fix coming in 2 weeks — do not promise it's live)
- No multi-retailer coverage (Walmart-only for Phase 0)
- Do not say "we're like MMM" — say "we answer the questions MMM can't, at the shelf level"
