## Product Architecture
Unified Retail Ecommerce Management (REM) platform:
- Sales Analytics
- Retail Media Management (RMM) — includes iROAS/incrementality module
- Digital Shelf (content, availability, competitive monitoring)
- AllyAI — AI agents for automation (76% account adoption as of Q4 2025)

## Critical Product Moves (2025-2026)
**December 2025 — Standalone Incrementality Estimation Module**
*Most important DSIM-relevant event.*
- Launched as an independently purchasable product — no longer requires full RMM subscription
- Quote: *"Brands that do not currently subscribe to the RMM platform can also access the Incrementality Estimation Module as an independent solution for measurement, reporting and planning purposes."*
- Methodology: Observational — proprietary neural network analyzing share of voice, shopper propensity, keyword relevance
- Attribution window: 14 days (keyword-level, search only)
- NOT a holdout/test-and-control methodology
- Does not incorporate shelf signals (availability, SoS, content) as inputs

**March 2026 — Full AI Agents Suite ("biggest launch in company history")**
- Agents across Sales, Digital Shelf, Content, Retail Media
- Positioning: agency replacement ("10-100x better than traditional agencies")
- Level I and Level II capability tiers

**May 2025 — AllyAI suite expanded (Europe focus)**
- New AllyAI capabilities targeting EU retail channels (Amazon EU) to offset US tariff pressure
- Geographic expansion priority, not Walmart-specific measurement depth

## Methodology Gap — The Core DSIM Differentiator
CommerceIQ's own November 2025 blog: *"CommerceIQ's incrementality tool is focused on search and keyword-based incrementality, based on 14-day direct sales conversions due to specific advertising activities."*

Walmart Connect's April 2025 native Search Incrementality product explicitly positioned this methodology type as inferior: *"gold-standard test-and-control methodology backed by real, verified sales data"* vs. *"third-party data or observational models."*

Walmart is publicly undermining the methodology CIQ uses — on Walmart's own platform.

## Activation Conflict (Structural — Not Yet Publicly Named)
The conflict of interest exists structurally: CIQ's incrementality model is designed to optimize their activation engine. No external analyst has publicly named this as a CommerceIQ-specific problem as of April 2026, but the structural conditions are documented and articulable.

## DSIM Displacement Narrative
1. **Methodology challenge:** "Can you show your CFO a MAPE and R² for their incrementality output? We can. CIQ doesn't publish those."
2. **Independence:** "CIQ measures incrementality to optimize CIQ's bids. We have no activation stake — the number isn't influenced by what we want you to spend."
3. **Complement where CIQ is already deployed:** Position DSIM as the independent audit layer above CIQ's optimization. Don't try to displace.
4. **Scope:** "CIQ measures keyword-level, 14-day, search-only. DSIM decomposes across 6 shelf levers at the store-cluster level, always-on."

## DSIM Competitive Risks from CommerceIQ
- Their standalone module validates the measurement-only market — more buyers will ask for this
- 38% YoY growth + 2,200+ brands means they will reach accounts before DSIM
- If CIQ invests in holdout/test-and-control methodology, the methodology gap narrows
- The $25K entry point may pressure DSIM's $50K PoC pricing in some accounts
