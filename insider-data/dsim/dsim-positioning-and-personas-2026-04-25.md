## 2. The Positioning Decision: What Leadership Needs to Agree On

The transcript surfaced three competing framings of what DSIM *is*. Leadership couldn't resolve this on the call. The next session must produce one answer. These are the options, with honest trade-offs:

### Option A: "Retail Media Measurement Through the Digital Shelf Lens"
**One-liner:** "The only measurement that tells you whether your Walmart Connect investment is working against a shelf problem — before you spend another dollar."
**Buyer:** eCommerce / Retail Media team
**Advantage:** Tight, defensible, directly connected to a measurable line item (Walmart Connect budget)
**Risk:** Narrows DSIM to retail media optimization; misses the finance/RGM buyer; competes more directly with Incremental.com and CIQ on their own turf

### Option B: "Sales Decomposition for Every Lever, Not Just Media"
**One-liner:** "For the first time, see how much of your Walmart sales came from availability, content quality, Share of Search, and media — separately, at the store cluster level."
**Buyer:** RGM / Finance / brand P&L owner
**Advantage:** Uncontested territory — no competitor can say this. Broader relevance across eCommerce, finance, and brand teams
**Risk:** Harder pitch; longer sales cycle; requires educating the buyer on what "lever decomposition" means; "sales decomposition" is not a purchase category anyone budgets for

### Option C: "Budget Reallocation Intelligence — Across Media AND Shelf"
**One-liner:** "Before your eCommerce team asks for more Walmart Connect budget, know whether the bottleneck is media spend or in-stock rate — and where to move the money if it is."
**Buyer:** Finance / VP RGM — the *approver* of eCommerce budget requests
**Advantage:** Creates a concrete, monetizable use case; positions DSIM as the CFO's answer to "should I approve this budget ask?"; taps into the natural tension between eCommerce (spend more) and finance (prove ROI first)
**Risk:** May feel adversarial to the eCommerce team (the user); requires a two-stakeholder sale; budget reallocation is not yet fully explicit in the product

**Recommendation:** Option B as the *what* (the umbrella definition); Option C as the *wedge* for finance buyers; Option A as the *use case* for eCommerce buyers. The core pitch is B, but the first conversation changes by persona. This matches how content optimization is sold at DataWeave — one product, persona-specific value statements.

## 3. Persona Definitions — The Three Buyers

These are defined from first principles using: the Lactalis engagement data in the transcript, the ICP brand list, the IAB research Jeeva surfaced, and known CPG org structures. **Validate each with CSMs before the next leadership session.**

---

### Persona 1: The eCommerce / Retail Media Leader
**Title:** VP eCommerce, Director of Retail Media, Senior Manager Walmart Channel
**Reports to:** Chief eCommerce Officer or VP Sales
**Team size:** 3–15 people; includes campaign managers, digital shelf analysts, sometimes a dedicated Walmart Connect team
**Budget authority:** Owns Walmart Connect budget ($500K–$5M+/year for Tier 1 CPGs); does NOT control trade spend or broader media budget

#### Day-to-day reality
- Pulls Walmart Connect dashboards every day. ROAS is the primary metric they're judged on by their manager.
- Has a weekly or biweekly review with the retail media agency (or Publicis/Pacvue/CIQ) that manages their campaigns.
- Manually monitors Share of Search and in-stock rates — usually in separate tools, not connected to media performance.
- Hears "your ROAS went up" from the agency but can't explain to their VP why sales didn't move proportionally.
- Gets blamed when a media campaign underperforms, even if the real constraint was OOS during peak.
- Frequently makes budget requests to Finance that take 4–6 weeks to approve.

#### KPIs they're measured on
1. Walmart.com net sales (primary)
2. ROAS / iROAS on Walmart Connect (reported by agency or platform)
3. Share of Search (SoS) in category
4. In-stock rate (OSA) during campaign windows
5. Market share (Walmart channel vs. competition)
6. CPC and budget efficiency

#### KRs they're trying to hit this quarter
- Hit $X in Walmart.com net sales (specific to their brand/category)
- Achieve ≥ X% in-stock rate during next major promotional window
- Grow Share of Search from X% to Y% in primary category
- Demonstrate that Walmart Connect investment is generating net-new sales (not just capturing organic demand)

#### What DSIM means for this persona
DSIM tells them something their current tools cannot: **whether a media campaign underperformed because of a media problem or a shelf problem**. Specifically:
- "Your July campaign ROAS dropped because 31% of your Sponsored Search impressions hit stores where you were below 70% in-stock. Media wasn't the issue."
- "Your Offsite Display iROI is 1.55 vs. 0.83 for Onsite Search. You have headroom to move $30K/day from Onsite Search into Offsite Display before you hit diminishing returns."
- "17 store clusters showed materially different response rates. Your Kansas City cluster has 3× the media ROI of your Dallas cluster — and zero availability issues."

#### One-liner for this persona
"You get ROAS from Walmart Connect. DSIM tells you whether that ROAS was suppressed by your in-stock rate — and which stores to fix before your next campaign launches."

#### Likely objections from this persona
- "I already have Walmart Connect dashboards / Scintilla / CIQ — what does this add?" → DSIM connects them. No existing tool shows how SoS, OSA, content quality, and media spend interact to produce incremental sales.
- "This sounds like another consulting engagement that takes 3 months to get an output." → PoC is 6–8 weeks, Tableau delivery. Not a report — a reallocation recommendation.
- "I don't control my shelf metrics — that's the supply chain team." → True, but DSIM gives you the dollar value of fixing it. Now you have the business case to take to supply chain.

### Persona 2: The Finance / Revenue Growth Management (RGM) Leader
**Title:** VP Revenue Growth Management, Director Commercial Finance, VP Trade Marketing
**Reports to:** CFO or Chief Commercial Officer
**Team size:** 2–8 analysts; analytical, spreadsheet-heavy, skeptical of platform-generated numbers
**Budget authority:** Reviews and approves or redirects eCommerce budget requests; controls trade spend allocation; has P&L visibility that eCommerce team does not

#### Day-to-day reality
- Receives budget requests from eCommerce team ("we need $2M more in Walmart Connect to hit share targets") and has no independent way to evaluate whether that's the right lever.
- Uses NielsenIQ / Circana for category-level trend analysis. Sees results 6–8 weeks after the fact.
- Runs trade spend ROI analysis but it's backward-looking and doesn't separate media lift from organic baseline.
- Frequently in tension with eCommerce team: eCommerce says "more media," finance says "prove it worked first."
- Increasingly asked by leadership to develop a view on "what's the right mix between trade, media, pricing, and assortment investment?"
- Familiar with MMM from offline media days but skeptical it applies to retail media (and the IAB research Jeeva surfaced confirms this skepticism is industrywide).

#### KPIs they're measured on
1. Gross margin / trade spend efficiency
2. Incremental revenue per trade dollar invested
3. Revenue attribution accuracy (how much of sales growth is truly incremental vs. baseline)
4. JBP target achievement (Joint Business Plan commitments to Walmart)
5. Category contribution to P&L

#### KRs they're trying to hit this quarter
- Reduce misallocated trade and media spend by X%
- Produce a defensible media ROI model for internal finance review
- Build a framework to evaluate eCommerce budget requests with independent data
- Demonstrate to leadership that channel investment decisions are grounded in causal analysis, not correlation

#### What DSIM means for this persona
DSIM is the **independent audit layer** that finance teams have never had for retail media. Specifically:
- "Before you approve another $500K in Walmart Connect, here's what DSIM shows: 63% of your current Onsite Search budget is in the saturation zone (0.83 iROI). Moving $X to Offsite Display would generate more incremental sales from the same budget."
- "Your eCommerce team's media underperformed in Q3 not because of budget — your in-stock rate was 72% during peak and suppressed lift by an estimated $X. Fix availability first."
- "DSIM output is vendor-neutral. Unlike your agency's ROAS numbers, this is the number you can defend to your CFO and take into the JBP."

#### The finance angle the transcript surfaced (Karthik's insight — verbatim)
> "If we go and ask an e-commerce team that you know what you're spending $5,000 a day on retail media, in our view, you should be just spending $3,000. They are not going to cut back and say I already have this $2,000 budget with me. I'm not going to let that go away. So unless it's about reallocation."

This is the correct framing. DSIM's pitch to finance is NOT "cut their budget." It's: **"Make sure the money flows to the lever that generates the most incremental return — whether that's media or fixing a shelf problem."** Finance can take credit for this reallocation as a strategic contribution, not a budget cut.

#### One-liner for this persona
"Your eCommerce team measures ROAS. DSIM tells you whether their results are constrained by a shelf problem the media budget can't fix — so you can make a defensible reallocation decision."

#### Likely objections from this persona
- "We use Analytic Partners / Circana for MMM — we already have measurement." → AP gives you the quarterly picture at the brand level. DSIM gives you the weekly shelf-level picture at the store cluster level. 5.9% MAPE vs. AP's 10–15% on the retail media layer. Complementary, not competitive.
- "The data is too granular for my team to act on." → Tableau delivery is configured to the decisions your team makes. The output is: "here's where to reallocate, here's the expected dollar impact."
- "I don't have budget for another measurement tool." → DSIM is a $50–75K PoC scoped as a project, not a SaaS subscription. Category manager budget line, not IT procurement. Lactalis identified $30K/day in reallocation opportunity — PoC pays for itself before delivery closes.

### Persona 3: The Brand / Category Manager (Secondary Persona)
**Title:** Senior Brand Manager, Category Director, VP Marketing (Walmart-focused)
**Reports to:** Chief Marketing Officer or VP Brand
**Team size:** 2–6; includes shopper marketing, field sales support
**Budget authority:** Shopper marketing budget and promotional calendar; does NOT control retail media directly

#### Day-to-day reality
- Owns the brand's Walmart P&L and the promotional calendar (display windows, price promotions, sampling).
- Gets category performance reports from NielsenIQ / Circana but they're 6–8 weeks lagged.
- Monitors digital shelf metrics inconsistently — usually through Profitero or DataWeave DSA, but rarely connects shelf changes to sales impact.
- Cares deeply about competitive threats: "which competitor is gaining search share in my category, and should I respond?"
- Frequently in planning cycles for new SKU launches and needs to predict Walmart channel performance before committing to launch.
- Manages shopper marketing agency (separate from retail media agency).

#### KPIs they're measured on
1. Category market share (Walmart channel)
2. Brand/SKU velocity (units per store per week)
3. Distribution / ACV (All Commodity Volume)
4. Promotional ROI (lift during display / price promotion windows)
5. New SKU sell-through rate
6. Content compliance and ratings

#### KRs they're trying to hit this quarter
- Defend or grow category share against a specific competitor launching new SKUs
- Prove promotional lift justifies trade investment (JBP target)
- Achieve X% ACV on new SKU within 90 days of launch
- Identify and close content/assortment gaps before peak selling season

#### What DSIM means for this persona
DSIM gives the brand team **digital shelf intelligence that is causally connected to sales**, not just monitoring. Specifically:
- "Your competitor gained 3 points of Share of Search in the past 6 weeks in the Snacks category. DSIM estimates this is suppressing your Walmart.com sales by $X/week. Here's which store clusters are most affected."
- "Your new SKU launch had strong media support but 67% content compliance at launch — DSIM estimates this content gap cost $X in the first 30 days."
- "Your display promotion in June produced a +18% sales lift in 9 of 17 store clusters. In the remaining 8, OOS during the promotion window neutralized the lift entirely."

#### Why this is a secondary persona
This persona *consumes* DSIM outputs but is less likely to be the budget holder for the engagement. The eCommerce team or RGM team purchases DSIM; the brand team benefits from the outputs. Position DSIM to eCommerce/RGM teams but include the brand team use case in the deck to show breadth of organizational value.

#### One-liner for this persona
"By the time Nielsen tells you a competitor took two points of share, it happened 6 weeks ago. DSIM shows you the shelf signal that preceded it — while there's still time to respond."

## 4. The Missing Proof: DSA KPI ROI Independent of DSIM

Sangeet raised this correctly in the transcript and it's the most technically important gap. Paraphrasing his point:

> "Even if you leave DSIM aside for a minute — are we clear on the ROI for DSA KPIs (Share of Search, content quality, OSA, ratings) themselves? Because if we can't establish that, then the DSIM output feels theoretical."

This is right. The DSIM model decomposes sales lift across levers. But if a brand's finance team asks "how do you know improving content quality from 70 to 80 is worth $X?" — we need a standalone answer that doesn't require believing the DSIM model output.

**What's needed:** A benchmarking analysis that shows, across the Lactalis data and any other brand-level data available:
- For every 1-point improvement in Share of Search → estimated sales lift (with confidence interval)
- For every X% OOS during campaign → estimated lift suppression
- For every content quality score improvement (e.g., 70→80) → estimated conversion rate lift and downstream sales impact

This becomes the **credibility substrate** for the DSIM model. Without it, DSIM's output is defensible only at the "total lift" level (R²=91% model fit), not at the individual lever level. This analysis doesn't require a new PoC — it can be done on Lactalis data and reported as a proof point.

**Priority:** HIGH. Schedule as a next engineering/data science sprint before any new customer PoC.

## 5. The Budget Reallocation Gap

The transcript made this explicit: DSIM currently shows that Offsite Display has a higher iROI than Onsite Search, but does not say "take $X from Onsite Search and move it to Offsite Display." The model shows the point of diminishing returns but not the cross-channel reallocation recommendation explicitly.

**What's needed in the product:**
- Output layer that explicitly states: "Move $[X]/day from [Channel A] to [Channel B] to maximize incremental return within your current total budget"
- Show the before/after iROI and predicted incremental sales impact of the move
- Include the "saturation ceiling" per channel — how much can you move before hitting diminishing returns in the new channel?

**This is an MVP-level product gap.** The model has the data to do this — it's a presentation/output layer decision, not a modeling problem. This should be in the next Lactalis delivery and in any future PoC output.

**Immediate fix (no engineering required):** Add a "Recommended Reallocation" section to the Tableau dashboard for Lactalis showing the explicit dollar move. Use this in the next client meeting to address their "they didn't love the incremental ROI analysis" feedback.

## 6. Research Agenda — Where to Go Deeper

### 6.1 Competitive Intelligence Gaps

**Citrus Ad (Publicis platform) — HIGH PRIORITY**
Lactalis uses Publicis managing their retail media via Citrus Ad. This is a white-label self-service retail media platform Publicis acquired. The critical finding from the transcript: Publicis has NOT successfully integrated Profitero shelf data into Citrus Ad. This is our opening.
- What to research: Citrus Ad's feature set, data sources, retailer coverage, how it handles incrementality measurement
- Where to look: citrussad.com, Publicis annual reports, trade press on the acquisition
- DSIM pitch against Citrus Ad: "Publicis optimizes your Walmart Connect campaigns through Citrus Ad. They can't integrate the shelf data that determines whether that media spend has room to work. DataWeave gives them the shelf layer they're missing — and gives you the vendor-neutral view of whether the whole system is performing."

**IAB Anti-MMM Research (Jeeva surfaced) — HIGH PRIORITY**
Three IAB reports are directly relevant to DSIM positioning:
1. April 2024 IAB blog by Colin Colburn (VP): "MMM was designed for a different media environment and produces output that systematically misattributes sales"
2. October 2024: "IAB Retail Media Advance Measurement and Data Collaboration" — Walmart Connect head states MMM is "not technically causal" for retail media
3. November 2025: "Guidelines for Incremental Measurement in Commerce Media" — rates MMM as "moderate to weak causal strength" for retail media
- These reports are on iab.com and should be linked directly in the competitive landscape as third-party validation of DSIM's positioning
- Note: Eddie is cautious about leaning on MMM criticism (doesn't want to be seen as "anti-MMM" — he wants to differentiate). Suggested framing: use IAB to validate the *problem* (measurement gap in retail media), not to attack MMM as a category. DSIM's answer is not "instead of MMM" — it's "in addition to MMM, specifically for retail media at the shelf level."
- This is also the Dentsu + Northwestern University research thread Jeeva mentioned — find these reports and extract the retail media measurement gap framing.

**Keyword Discovery Gap — MEDIUM PRIORITY**
Raised in the transcript: DSIM can tell you which keywords you ARE bidding on and their iROI, but cannot yet tell you which keywords you SHOULD be bidding on that you're currently missing. This is an adjacent product capability:
- Data exists (Walmart Connect API has search query volume data; DataWeave's SoS model tracks keyword-level competition)
- The gap is connecting DataWeave's Share of Search data to Walmart Connect keyword bidding recommendations
- For current positioning: frame this as a roadmap item, not a current gap. "DSIM currently optimizes within your campaign mix. Keyword discovery is on the product roadmap for Phase 2."
- Research needed: how do Pacvue and CIQ handle keyword discovery for Walmart? What's their methodology?

### 6.3 Multi-Retailer Feasibility

The user raised this as a strategic question. Here's the honest assessment:

**Technical feasibility:** High. The DSIM model (Bayesian MMM + GAM + PCA + Random Forest) is retailer-agnostic. The architecture only changes in the data inputs.

**Data availability by retailer:**
| Retailer | Shelf data (DataWeave) | Sales data | Media/ad data | DSIM feasibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Walmart | ✅ Production-grade | Walmart Luminate | Walmart Connect API | **Live (PoC complete)** |
| Amazon | ✅ Production-grade | Brand Analytics (aggregated, not SKU-transaction) | Amazon Attribution API | **Medium** — Brand Analytics less granular than Luminate |
| Kroger | ✅ Coverage | 84.51° (available to brand partners) | 8451 Stratum | **High** — 84.51° is the most brand-accessible granular sales dataset outside Walmart |
| Target | ✅ Coverage | Roundel (limited) | Target Roundel API | **Low-medium** — Target's data access is more restricted |
| Costco | Limited | Very limited | No self-serve Connect equivalent | **Low** — Costco data access is poor |

**Recommendation:** Kroger as the second retailer after Walmart. 84.51°'s transaction-level data is the closest analogue to Walmart Luminate, and DataWeave has shelf coverage. A Kroger DSIM PoC is technically feasible within 6 months of Walmart PoC stabilization.

**What changes in a multi-retailer pitch:**
- The retailer comparison use case emerges: "Is your Walmart media generating more incremental lift than your Kroger media — and why?"
- The brand-level buyer becomes more relevant: the eCommerce Manager who owns both Walmart and Kroger channel will pay more for a tool that spans both
- The store-cluster approach needs recalibration per retailer (Kroger's cluster structure differs from Walmart's)

**For current positioning:** Walmart-first is correct. Do NOT market multi-retailer until the Kroger data model is validated. But mention it as roadmap to prevent "is this Walmart-only forever?" objections from enterprise buyers.

## 7. CSM Validation Framework

Before the next leadership session, run these questions by 2–3 CSMs managing existing DataWeave DSA accounts that are also Walmart Connect spenders. These brands are likely DSIM ICP and their behavior today is the ground truth we need.

### Questions for CSMs (5-6 minutes per conversation)

1. **On measurement today:** "When you're in your QBR with [brand], what does their Walmart performance conversation focus on? Is it ROAS, sales numbers, market share — and who in the room is responsible for it?"

2. **On the eCommerce/finance tension:** "Has your brand ever asked for help explaining to their finance team whether their Walmart Connect spend is working? Or does the finance team typically just accept the agency's ROAS number?"

3. **On shelf + media connection:** "Does your brand ever try to connect what's happening on the shelf (in-stock rate, Share of Search changes) to what's happening with their media performance? Or do they treat those as separate conversations?"

4. **On budget decisions:** "When your brand's eCommerce team wants more Walmart Connect budget — who approves that? And what evidence do they need to show?"

5. **On persona:** "At the brands you work with, who would be most interested in knowing that a content quality improvement from 70 to 80 was worth $X in incremental Walmart sales — the eCommerce team, the brand manager, or someone in finance?"

6. **On multi-retailer:** "Is your brand primarily Walmart-focused, or are they running similar campaigns on Kroger or Amazon? If we could show them this type of analysis across multiple retailers, who would care most about that?"

### What to do with CSM input
- If 2+ CSMs say "finance doesn't look at ROAS, they just approve what eCommerce asks for" → de-prioritize Persona 2 as the primary buyer, position Persona 1 first
- If 2+ CSMs say "the brand team cares most about competitive shelf moves" → elevate Persona 3
- If 2+ CSMs say "they're asking about Kroger / Amazon too" → accelerate multi-retailer roadmap conversation
