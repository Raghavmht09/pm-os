# DSIM ICP Target List: Top 50 CPG/FMCG Brands on Walmart
**Target Objective:** Identify ideal test/launch clients for DataWeave's DSIM platform based on Walmart channel dependency, online retail media spend, and digital shelf volatility.
**Last updated:** April 17, 2026

## Methodology & ICP Fit
The Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) for DSIM's initial Walmart phase follows these criteria:
- **Baseline Requirement:** High percentage of US revenue dependent on Walmart.com / omnichannel sales.
- **Retail Media Spender:** Active, high-volume participants in Walmart Connect API (essential for DSIM to pull media inputs).
- **Shelf Complexity:** Operates in categories with high volatility in Share of Search (SOS) and competitive pricing dynamics (necessitating DSIM's digital shelf correlation).
- **Reference MVP (Lactalis):** As learned from earlier POCs, brands with high-velocity grocery, beverage, and dairy dependencies heavily benefit from store-level cluster measurement to manage out-of-stock (OOS) rates alongside promotions.

Below are the Top 50 Target Brands categorized by vertical. Due to corporate hierarchies, closing one Parent Company often unlocks multiple brands below.

## Actionable Strategy for DSIM Rollout
- **Cluster Focus:** Brands 1-17 (Food & Bev) are highly susceptible to "phantom out-of-stocks" (when a product is shown online but unavailable at the local store cluster). DSIM's unique store-clustering capability is the strongest wedge for these accounts.
- **SOS Focus:** Brands 18-31 (Personal Care) suffer from shelf cannibalization via heavy Retail Media spending. DSIM's ability to isolate organic SOS from paid visibility is critical here.
