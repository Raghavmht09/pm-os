**Scope:** Fashion/Apparel, Consumer Electronics, Home/DIY — feasibility of expanding DSIM beyond CPG
**Use:** Long-term TAM expansion strategy

## Fashion / Apparel

### How Brands Operate
Seasonal collections (4–6 drops per year). Annual planning around **OTB (Open-To-Buy) budgets**, **sell-through targets** (65–75% full-price before markdown), **markdown cadence** (first markdown ~6 weeks for fast fashion, ~10–12 weeks for premium). Wholesale brands negotiate door counts, MAP policies, co-op marketing 6–9 months ahead. Omnichannel split: DTC-led for premium ($100+ AUR), wholesale-led for mass.

### Measurement Tools Today
**Attribution-platform heavy, MMM-light.** DTC brands use **Northbeam, Triple Whale, Rockerbox** for last-click + multi-touch on Meta/TikTok/Google. Wholesale-side measurement primitive: sell-through reports from retailer EDI feeds, manual co-op ROI in spreadsheets. Influencer attribution via **CreatorIQ, Tagger, LTK**. Enterprise houses (LVMH, Tapestry, PVH) starting to deploy **Nielsen MMM or Analytic Partners** but at portfolio level, not SKU-cluster.

### Retail Media Exposure
**Meta + TikTok dominant** (50–70% of digital). Retail media fragmented: Amazon Fashion Ads, Macy's Media Network, Nordstrom Media Network, Target Roundel (mass), Saks Media. Department store RMNs still small (<$200M each [VERIFY]) vs. Amazon Ads' fashion vertical. TikTok Shop now real channel for emerging brands.

### Digital Shelf Signals That Matter
- Image quality + count (8+ images, model + flat lay + detail)
- **Size availability matrix** (XS through XXL completeness)
- Color variant completeness
- **Fit information** (model height/size, "true to size" reviews)
- **Returns rate** (silent killer — fashion returns 25–40% online)
- Review sentiment on fit (more important than star rating)

### DSIM Lever Applicability
**6-lever model partially holds:**
- **MORE important:** Content quality (images, fit info), Competitive pressure (markdown timing vs. competitors)
- **LESS important:** Availability — replace with size/color matrix completeness
- **NEW levers needed:** Returns rate as anti-lever, Markdown depth/timing, Influencer/UGC velocity, Sell-through pace
- Share of Search real but Pinterest/TikTok search matter as much as Google/Amazon

### Buyer Persona
- DTC-led: VP DTC / Chief Digital Officer + Director of Performance Marketing
- Wholesale-led: VP Wholesale + Director of Retail Channel Marketing
- Heritage house: CMO heavily involved — fashion CMOs unusually senior in MMM decisions

Decision committee larger than CPG — merchandising has a vote.

### DSIM Acceptance Level
**MEDIUM-LOW.** Bifurcated maturity: DTC over-tooled on attribution (don't think they need MMM), wholesale under-measured but skeptical of "data tools." Frustration signal exists around **wholesale co-op ROI** and **markdown decision support** — that's the wedge.

### Pitch Framing
- **One-liner:** *"Decompose what's actually driving sell-through at every door — markdown, content, search, or competitor — before you cut your next OTB."*
- **Top problems DSIM solves:** (1) Wholesale co-op marketing ROI is a black box; (2) Markdown timing decisions made on gut, not lift modeling
- **Top objections:** (1) "We already have Triple Whale / Northbeam"; (2) "Fashion is too creative-driven for a Bayesian model to capture"

## Consumer Electronics

### How Brands Operate
**12–18 month product lifecycle** with brutal price decay (15–25% MSRP erosion in months 6–12). Annual planning around **launch windows** (CES Jan, IFA Sept, back-to-school, holiday) and **channel allocation** (Best Buy vs. Amazon vs. Costco vs. DTC). Heavy reliance on **MAP enforcement** and **regional channel partner programs**. Reviews drive everything — a bad RTINGS or Wirecutter score can kill a launch.

### Measurement Tools Today
**Most MMM-mature of the three verticals.** Samsung, LG, Sony, Bose, Sonos all run **Nielsen MMM, Analytic Partners, or in-house Bayesian models**. Heavy use of product-level demand forecasting (SAS, Blue Yonder), regional pricing analytics (DataWeave already credible here via DSA), review velocity tracking (Bazaarvoice, PowerReviews). Sell-out data via **NPD/Circana** is industry standard.

### Retail Media Exposure
- **Amazon Ads massive** (40–60% of digital retail spend for CE)
- **Best Buy Ads (Best Buy Media Network)** — second largest specialty RMN [VERIFY]
- Costco (limited but premium)
- Manufacturer DTC sites (samsung.com, lg.com — significant own-channel spend)
- Newegg, B&H for enthusiast segments
- Less Meta/TikTok than fashion; more YouTube + review platform sponsorships

### Digital Shelf Signals That Matter
- **Price competitiveness across retailers** (#1 — MAP violations, price gaps)
- Review velocity + recency (2-year-old review is dead weight)
- Technical spec accuracy + completeness (wrong refresh rate = returns)
- Bundle composition (HDMI cable included? Wall mount?)
- Star rating + review count thresholds (4.2+ stars, 500+ reviews is "buy zone")

### DSIM Lever Applicability
**6-lever model largely holds and fits well:**
- **MORE important:** Pricing (dominant — price elasticity is huge in CE), Competitive pressure (cross-retailer price gaps)
- **LESS important:** Availability rarely the issue (CE rarely stocks out except at launch)
- **NEW levers needed:** Review velocity, MAP compliance, Bundle/promo structure, Launch-window timing

Share of Search on Amazon is critical — Amazon is half the market.

### Buyer Persona
- Director Channel Marketing (owns retailer-specific spend)
- Trade Marketing Director (owns co-op + MDF)
- VP Sales – North America (oversees Best Buy/Amazon/Costco relationships)
- Regional ops for in-store / endcap decisions

Less CMO involvement than fashion; more channel/sales-led.

### DSIM Acceptance Level
**HIGHEST of the three.** Already MMM-literate, already buying measurement tools, already feel pain on cross-retailer pricing chaos. Frustration signal real around **retail media ROI fragmentation** (Amazon vs. Best Buy vs. Costco vs. own-site — nobody decomposes this well). DataWeave has existing credibility via DSA.

### Pitch Framing
- **One-liner:** *"The only model that decomposes lift across Amazon, Best Buy, Costco, and your own DTC — with price elasticity, MAP violations, and review velocity as first-class levers."*
- **Top problems DSIM solves:** (1) Retail media spend allocated across 4+ RMNs with no unified ROI view; (2) Price elasticity by store cluster invisible in current MMM
- **Top objections:** (1) "We already run Nielsen MMM at portfolio level"; (2) "Our measurement is sell-out via Circana, not retailer-level lift"

## Home / DIY

### How Brands Operate
**Project-based and dual-audience:** Pro contractors (40–55% of revenue for brands like DeWalt, Sherwin-Williams) and DIYers (volume but lower basket). Annual planning around **spring selling season (Mar–Jun)**, **Pro loyalty programs** (Home Depot Pro Xtra, Lowe's MVPs Pro), **project bundles** (kitchen reno, deck build). Less seasonal than CPG/fashion but **highly weather-dependent regionally**. Bulk pricing for Pro structurally different from DIY pricing.

### Measurement Tools Today
**Least MMM-mature of the three.** Heavy reliance on trade marketing spreadsheets, contractor program ROI (loyalty redemption rates), in-store project demo attendance, co-op marketing reconciliation. A few large brands (Stanley Black & Decker, Sherwin-Williams) run portfolio MMM via Nielsen/Analytic Partners but rarely at SKU-cluster. **Contractor program measurement is a known gap** — brands struggle to attribute Pro Xtra spend to incremental sales.

### Retail Media Exposure
- **Home Depot Retail Media (Orange Apron Media)** — fastest-growing specialty RMN [VERIFY]
- Lowe's One Roof Media Network (rebranded from Lowe's Advertising) [VERIFY]
- Amazon (smaller share than CE, but growing for tools)
- Manufacturer DTC small — most brands don't sell direct
- Less Meta/TikTok; more **HGTV, YouTube DIY creators, contractor trade pubs**

### Digital Shelf Signals That Matter
- **In-stock by store / by zip code** (#1 — projects are immediate, "I need it today")
- Bulk / case pack availability (Pro need 50 boxes of screws, not 1)
- **Pro vs. DIY pricing visibility** (often hidden behind Pro login)
- Project bundle completeness (does listing show compatible accessories?)
- Local store inventory accuracy (worst pain point — listed in-stock, actually not)

### DSIM Lever Applicability
**6-lever model fits well, with geo-emphasis:**
- **MORE important:** Availability (geographic — store-cluster is the natural unit, **DSIM's strength**), Retail media (HD + Lowe's are the spend)
- **LESS important:** Share of Search weaker (project searches happen on HD.com / Lowes.com / YouTube, less on Google)
- **NEW levers needed:** Pro program enrollment lift, Weather/seasonality regional, Project bundle attach rate, Contractor vs. DIY mix

**Store-cluster modeling is the BEST FIT of the three verticals** — DataWeave's existing HD relationship is gold.

### Buyer Persona
- VP Pro Sales (owns contractor channel — increasingly the primary persona)
- Director of Retail Channel (HD/Lowe's relationship owner)
- Director Trade Marketing (co-op + MDF)

Dual contractor + DIY ownership creates internal alignment friction — but also a clear wedge for measurement that decomposes both.

### DSIM Acceptance Level
**MEDIUM-HIGH.** Measurement maturity low (good for DSIM — less competition from incumbents), frustration signal high (Pro program ROI is real boardroom issue), and **DataWeave's existing HD relationship via DSA is a credibility shortcut**.

### Pitch Framing
- **One-liner:** *"The first measurement system that decomposes Pro vs. DIY lift by store cluster — across Orange Apron, Lowe's One Roof, and your trade marketing dollars."*
- **Top problems DSIM solves:** (1) Pro program ROI is a black box; (2) Geographic store-cluster lift is invisible in current MMM
- **Top objections:** (1) "Our HD/Lowe's reps already give us sell-through reports"; (2) "We don't run MMM at all today — too big a leap"
