# ICP Buyer Behavior: How Tier 1 CPG Teams Measure Walmart Performance Today
**Research date:** April 25, 2026
**Purpose:** Ground truth on how DataWeave's target buyers (eCommerce, RGM/Finance, Brand teams) actually operate — to validate DSIM personas and sharpen sales conversations
**Sources:** Bain & Company, Skai, IAB, Digiday, Walmart Data Ventures, Groceryshop 2025 speaker profiles, LinkedIn job postings

---

## 1. The Typical Walmart Measurement Stack at a Tier 1 CPG

The measurement stack is not unified — it's a patchwork of tools governed by different teams with different budget owners.

**Core data layer:**
- **Walmart Scintilla** (formerly Luminate): The primary first-party data source. CPG brands use it for sales, category penetration, customer basket data, in-stock rates, and web traffic. Large CPGs — including Kraft Heinz and PepsiCo — are active Scintilla subscribers. [Verified: Walmart Data Ventures, October 2024; Modern Retail, 2025]
- **Walmart Connect dashboards:** Campaign-level ROAS, attributed sales, and impression data for sponsored search and display. Self-reported. Supplier-facing only.

**Retail media execution and optimization:**
- **Pacvue:** Named explicitly in Kraft Heinz, Campbell Soup, and Publicis Commerce job postings as a required tool. Used for bid management, campaign execution, and cross-retailer reporting across Walmart, Amazon, and Target. [Verified: Built In/Campbell Soup job posting, 2025; Publicis SVP Commerce job posting, 2025]
- **Skai:** Second-tier tool at brands that need cross-retailer normalization. The Publicis SVP Commerce posting explicitly lists Pacvue and Skai as the standard agency-side toolkit. [Verified: Ziprecruiter/Publicis Groupe, 2025]
- **Stackline:** Listed as a "strong plus" in Campbell Soup's eCommerce Manager posting. Used for Share of Search, digital shelf scoring, and competitive benchmark tracking. [Verified: Built In, 2025]

**Independent shelf and competitive measurement:**
Tools like Profitero, DataWeave DSA, and Stackline fill the gap that Scintilla doesn't cover: Share of Search rank, content quality scores, in-stock rate benchmarked against competitors. Typically owned by the eCommerce/Digital Shelf team, not the media buying team.

**What's missing at most brands:**
An independent incrementality layer. Only 6% of advertisers report fully trusting retailers' own reported media metrics (Bain, 2024). Skai's 2025 State of Retail Media report found 36% of CPG marketers cite difficulty proving incrementality as a top challenge. [Verified: Bain, 2024; Skai, 2025]

## 2. KPIs a VP/Director of eCommerce Gets Measured On

Based on public job postings and Groceryshop 2025 speaker profiles:

**Sales and share:**
- Walmart.com net revenue growth (vs. plan and vs. prior year)
- Share of category at Walmart.com (absolute and trend)
- Share of Search rank in key search queries (tracked weekly via Stackline or Profitero)

**Digital shelf execution:**
- Content quality score / content compliance rate (title, images, A+ content, reviews)
- In-stock rate on Walmart.com
- New item setup speed and first-90-day velocity

**Retail media efficiency:**
- Return on Ad Spend from Walmart Connect campaigns (platform-reported, acknowledged as imperfect)
- Budget utilization vs. plan
- At more mature brands: incremental ROAS (iROAS), though few have continuous measurement capability [VERIFY: iROAS as formal review KPI — confirm with CPG-side customers]

**Strategic:**
- Walmart JBP target attainment
- Successful new item launches at Walmart.com
- Agency and vendor management against deliverables

**Named executives:** Andrea Steele (AVP eCommerce & Customer Marketing, Kraft Heinz) owns KHC's eCommerce and Omnichannel Marketing strategy. Vince Jones (SVP/GM Global eCommerce, PepsiCo) holds similar accountability at executive level. [Verified: Groceryshop 2025 speaker profiles]

## 3. How Finance/RGM Evaluates a Walmart Connect Budget Request

**The dominant pattern:** eCommerce teams submit requests using platform-reported ROAS. Finance/RGM teams are skeptical but lack an independent measurement framework. The result is negotiated approval based on category precedent and competitive necessity — not proven incrementality.

**What Finance actually wants to see** (per Skai's 2026 CPG formula research):
- Incremental ROAS, not attributed ROAS
- Contribution margin impact after all fees
- Distinction between "defensive spend" (holding Walmart Connect position) and "offensive spend" (generating genuine volume lift)

**What Finance actually receives:** Platform ROAS from Walmart Connect dashboards and Pacvue rollups.

**The structural tension:** Walmart now explicitly includes retail media spend targets in JBP negotiations as a condition of commercial partnership — making Walmart Connect budget a near-mandatory cost of doing business. This is why Finance teams are demanding independent incrementality measurement: they're being asked to commit eight-figure Walmart Connect budgets without an independent verification source. [Verified: Digiday, 2025]

**The IAB context:** MMM — Finance's traditional arbiter of media value — is structurally blind to closed-loop retail media signals. MMM systematically under-credits Walmart Connect performance, creating a structural argument Finance uses to cut budgets that may actually be working. [Verified: IAB white paper, April 7, 2026]

## 4. Where the Measurement Decision Lives

Ownership is split and contested:
- **eCommerce team:** Owns tool stack, campaign execution, and performance reporting
- **Shopper marketing / trade team:** Often holds the trade budget that Walmart JBP retail media commitments are funded from
- **Finance/RGM:** Sign-off authority on total investment levels; evaluates against MMM frameworks that don't accurately reflect retail media performance
- **Marketing/Brand:** Owns brand-funded display/CTV; frequently doesn't coordinate with eCommerce running sponsored search

**The most advanced brands** (Bain describes this as a "total commercial investment" approach) are building joint eCommerce-Finance-Shopper Marketing teams — but this remains the exception in 2025–2026. [VERIFY: No named brand publicly confirmed full integration — flag as structural claim]

## 5. The Quarterly Walmart JBP Review

**Who attends (CPG side):**
- VP/Director of eCommerce (owns digital agenda and Walmart.com performance data)
- National Account Manager / Customer Business Manager (owns physical shelf, promotion, and sales targets)
- Shopper Marketing lead (owns trade promotion budget and in-store execution)
- Finance/RGM representative (owns P&L, net revenue vs. plan)
- Occasionally: Supply Chain/Demand Planning (inventory and in-stock discussion)

**What data gets shown:**
- Walmart.com sales vs. plan and prior year (Scintilla-sourced)
- Walmart Connect campaign performance (ROAS, attributed sales, spend utilization)
- Digital shelf scorecard (content compliance, in-stock rate, SoS — pulled from Stackline or Profitero)
- Category rank and competitive share (Scintilla or Circana)
- New item launch velocity

**Key JBP tension:** Walmart pushed brands to increase Walmart Connect spend by ~25% YoY in 2025 JBPs despite flat or negative reported ROIs at some accounts. Finance teams have no independent verification source to push back or to validate. This is the structural problem DSIM is positioned to solve. [Verified: Digiday, 2025]
