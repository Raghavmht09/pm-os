# DSIM GTM Internal

Date: April 24, 2026

Summary

### Market Research: IAB Reports Criticizing MMM for Retail Media

- IAB (Interactive Advertising Bureau) published multiple reports criticizing MMM (Marketing Mix Modeling) for retail media measurement
- April 2024 IAB blog states "MMM was designed for a different media environment and produces output that systematically misattributes sales" for retail media
- Colin Colburn (IAB VP) has released three reports questioning MMM infrastructure for retail media
- October 2024 IAB report quotes Walmart Connect head describing MMM as "not technically causal" for retail media
- November 2025 IAB guidelines rate MMM as "moderate to weak causal strength" for retail media and "too slow for always-on campaign optimization"
- Reports from Dentsu and Northwestern University echo similar concerns about MMM for retail media
- US retail media spend reached $62B, representing 1 in 5 digital ad dollars
- Industry reports claim "retail media is maturing faster than the systems designed to measure it"

### Current Measurement Landscape and Gaps

- Three main measurement approaches exist: attribution frameworks (Amazon Attribution, Walmart Connect), MMM, and incremental testing
- Attribution measures correlation but not causation, operates in "walled gardens" that don't transfer knowledge across platforms
- Major blind spot: none of the current solutions measure digital shelf signals
- Organizations lack a single measurement framework everyone agrees on
- Different teams (brand, shopper, media, finance) have specific needs but no unified data set

### DSIM Positioning Challenges and Discussion

- Core positioning concept: "New way of measuring retail media with digital shelf metrics"
- Team debating whether DSIM is primarily for retail media optimization or broader sales decomposition
- Current analysis shows sales decomposition across pricing, content, availability, and retail media levers
- Key differentiation opportunity: measuring intangibles like content performance and availability impact that MMM doesn't capture
- Competitive positioning challenge: tools like Citrus Ad, Packview, and Sky already exist in market
- Need clearer messaging on budget reallocation vs. simple optimization

### Lactalis Client Insights

- Primary users are e-commerce team, with secondary users being retail media and Publicis teams
- Global retail media person recently joined and showed strong interest
- Lactalis uses Publicis (switched from Packview) for retail media management
- Publicis has Citrus Ad platform but hasn't successfully integrated Profitero digital shelf data
- Client questioned whether team can identify specific product gaps in assortment (e.g., which yogurt flavor to add)
- Lactalis didn't love initial incremental ROI analysis presentation

### Current Solution Capabilities and Gaps

- Team can analyze campaign types and recommend optimal spend levels
- Current analysis shows point of diminishing returns but doesn't explicitly show cross-channel reallocation
- Cannot yet identify keywords not being bid on that should be
- Keyword discovery happens during setup/onboarding phase
- Similar to assortment analysis: can identify gaps in coverage but not predict specific products to add
- Challenge establishing ROI for individual DSA KPIs independent of overall DSIM framework
- Difficulty attributing improvements to specific DSA actions taken by clients

### Strategic Questions Raised

- Should positioning target finance teams who control budgets vs. e-commerce teams who spend them?
- Finance angle: help finance teams evaluate if e-commerce requests for more retail media budget are justified or if other levers (availability, content, pricing) should be optimized first
- E-commerce teams unlikely to voluntarily reduce budgets even if analysis shows overspend
- Should solution focus on reallocation between channels vs. optimization within channels?
- Is Revenue Growth Management (RGM) team another potential persona to target?
- Concern about solution getting "watered down" if trying to address too many use cases

### Competitive Considerations

- Publicis has Citrus Ad but struggles with digital shelf integration
- Packview and Sky are established retail media optimization tools
- Profitero provides digital shelf data but isn't well integrated with retail media tools
- Partnership with MMM providers discussed but deemed premature without more proof points
