## Profitero

### Buyer Persona
**VP / Sr. Director of eCommerce** at a Tier-1 CPG (Mars, Nestlé, Kraft Heinz, Unilever profile). Reports to Chief Customer Officer or Chief Growth Officer. Day-to-day: owns digital shelf KPIs across Amazon + Walmart + Target + Kroger, runs weekly retailer scorecard, fields "why are we losing share at Walmart?" questions from the GM. Chose Profitero because **it's the incumbent digital-shelf default** — RFP-safe, Publicis-backed, integrated with their agency. **Funded out of eCommerce ops / digital shelf budget**, not measurement. Profitero+ measurement is upsold via Mars United Commerce as a services bolt-on.

### Daily User Persona
**eCommerce Analyst / Digital Shelf Manager** (3–6 yrs, often ex-agency). Opens Profitero every morning to check Share of Search, Available %, content compliance, price index per retailer. Workflow: export to Excel → reformat for Monday eComm sync → escalate OOS or content gaps to brokers/3PLs. Power users live in "Shelf Intelligence" and "Assortment"; rarely touch Profitero+ measurement modules.

### Feature Inventory (April 2026)
- **Shelf Intelligence Suite:** Share of Search, Search Analytics, Content Score, Ratings & Reviews, Availability, Assortment, Price & Promotion, MAP monitoring
- **Profitero+** (post-Mars United integration): Sales & Share (POS-fused), Activation Insights, Promo ROI, Retail Media benchmarks
- **Profitero Studio** (services layer): managed analytics, custom reporting, retailer-specific deep dives — delivered by Mars United Commerce consultants
- **Citrus Ad / Retail Media monitoring** (partner integrations)
- **AI Insights** ("Asora"-branded GenAI summarizer launched 2024) [VERIFY]
- Pricing: ACV typically $80K–$250K depending on retailer + brand count [VERIFY]

### Recent Launches & M&A (12 mo)

- **Q4 2025:** Profitero+ rebrand — measurement modules co-marketed with Mars United Commerce [VERIFY]
- **Sept 2025:** Expanded Walmart Connect data integration announced [VERIFY]

### Named Walmart Customer Outcomes
Public case studies skew Amazon-heavy. Walmart-specific named outcomes are scarce — most quantified results (Bayer, Mars, Kraft Heinz) are Amazon Share of Search lifts. **Searchable Walmart-named numerical case studies are weak — sales should not assume parity claims hold.**

### Pain Points (G2, TrustRadius, Reddit r/ecommerce)
- "Data is descriptive, not causal — tells me share dropped, not why"
- "Reports lag 24–72 hours; not usable for in-flight bid decisions"
- "Profitero+ measurement requires Pacvue or another causal layer to actually attribute media"
- "Cost has crept since Publicis acquisition; renewal pressure to bundle Mars United services"
- "Walmart coverage shallower than Amazon — store-level visibility limited" [VERIFY]

### DSIM Positioning vs. Profitero
**Displacement pitch:** *"Profitero tells you your shelf score dropped 4 points; DSIM tells you which of your 6 levers caused $1.2M in lost Walmart sales last quarter and which store clusters to fix first."*

- **DSIM wins** when: CPG already has digital shelf coverage and is asking "what drove the sales delta?" — measurement, not monitoring
- **Profitero wins** when: buyer needs cross-retailer shelf monitoring breadth (Amazon + Walmart + Target + Kroger + Instacart). DSIM is Walmart-only
- **Coexist:** DSIM as causal layer on top of Profitero's descriptive shelf feed — replaces the Pacvue dependency

## Skai

### Buyer Persona
**Director of Retail Media / Sr. Manager Paid Media** at a CPG or large agency. Reports to VP Media or VP Performance. Day-to-day: manages Amazon Ads, Walmart Connect, Criteo, Instacart bids across 50–500 SKUs; runs weekly pacing reviews; defends ROAS to CMO. Chose Skai because **it consolidates retail media bidding** across networks in one seat — historically strongest on Amazon Sponsored Products. **Funded from media ops / paid media budget.** Skai is a bidding tool that sells measurement; Profitero is a shelf tool that sells measurement.

### Daily User Persona
**Retail Media Specialist / Programmatic Buyer** (2–5 yrs). Lives in Skai 6+ hours/day: dayparting, keyword harvesting, negative-keyword pruning, budget reallocation, creative rotation. Buyer signs the contract, the specialist judges renewal. Skai's daily-user stickiness is real on Amazon and weakens visibly on Walmart.

### Feature Inventory (April 2026)
- **Retail Media:** Amazon, Walmart Connect, Instacart, Criteo, Target Roundel, Kroger Precision, Sam's Club MAP
- **Paid Search:** Google, Microsoft
- **Paid Social:** Meta, TikTok, Pinterest, Snap
- **App Marketing:** Apple Search Ads
- **Impact Navigator:** Skai's measurement / incrementality module (MMM-lite + experimental geo-tests)
- **Celerity:** AI bidding agent
- **Creative Center:** generative ad asset workflows
- **Published pricing:** $114K–$756K/year tiers (Essential / Advanced / Enterprise) [VERIFY]

### Recent Launches & M&A (12 mo)

- **Skai wins** when: buyer needs bid-level execution across Amazon + Walmart + 5 other networks. DSIM doesn't touch bidding
- **Coexist:** DSIM informs cross-lever budget allocation; Skai executes the media slice

## Incremental.com

### Buyer Persona
**VP Measurement / Head of Marketing Effectiveness** at a top-50 CPG (Bayer, Reckitt, Henkel profile). Reports to CMO or CFO. Day-to-day: owns MMM, incrementality testing, attribution; defends marketing ROI to finance; runs the annual budget allocation exercise. Chose Incremental because **WPP Connected Partner status (Sept 2025) + Walmart Connect official measurement partner (July 2024)** = pre-vetted, agency-approved, retailer-blessed. **Funded out of measurement / marketing analytics budget** — separate line from media ops.

### Daily User Persona
**Honestly: nobody opens it daily.** Incremental is a **quarterly-cadence MMM tool** — buyers and analysts engage during planning cycles (Q-end + budget season) and pull dashboards monthly. Closest to a daily user: **Marketing Science Analyst** running scenario planning, but realistic usage is 4–8 hours/week, not daily. Structural weakness — low daily stickiness vs. Skai/Profitero, but high renewal because output anchors annual budget conversations.

### Feature Inventory (April 2026)
- **Ensemble Causal Model** — proprietary blend of Bayesian MMM + causal inference + experimental design
- **Retail Media Measurement** (Walmart Connect, Amazon Ads, Criteo, Instacart)
- **Incrementality Testing** — geo-experiments, holdouts
- **Scenario Planner** — budget reallocation simulator
- **Connected Partner integrations:** WPP (Sept 2025), Walmart Connect (July 2024)
- **API / dashboard delivery** — no heavy front-end app; outputs flow to Looker/Tableau
- Pricing: ACV $150K–$500K typical for measurement-dedicated tools [VERIFY]
- **Critical limitation:** shelf signals (price, content, OSA, assortment) used as **control variables / noise to remove**, not as actionable levers in output. Buyer cannot ask "which store clusters should I fix content in?"

### Pain Points
- "Quarterly cadence — can't act on it intra-flight"

### DSIM Positioning vs. Incremental.com
**Displacement pitch:** *"Incremental treats your shelf as noise to control for; DSIM treats it as the lever to pull — same Bayesian rigor, but our output is 'fix content in these 47 store clusters,' not 'price elasticity = -1.4.'"*

- **DSIM wins** when: buyer wants **operational decomposition** at store-cluster level across all 6 levers — actionable, not academic
- **Incremental wins** when: buyer wants cross-retailer marketing ROI defense for the CFO at quarterly cadence. Incremental has the WPP + Walmart Connect badges DSIM doesn't yet have
- **Coexist:** DSIM operates the shelf-execution loop weekly; Incremental anchors the annual budget MMM. Different cadence, different buyer, possible tag-team sale

## Cross-Cutting Takeaway for DSIM GTM

The three competitors occupy distinct budget lines — **shelf ops (Profitero), media ops (Skai), measurement (Incremental)** — and DSIM has to pick which line it pulls from.

**Sharpest entry: measurement budget** with an Incremental-style pitch but **store-cluster operational output** that Incremental structurally can't produce because their model treats shelf as control, not signal. Profitero+/Pacvue and Skai Impact Navigator are the soft underbellies — both require a causal layer DSIM can be.
